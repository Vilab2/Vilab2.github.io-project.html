Subtopic 1: Online Fraud and Scams

Source 1: IC3 / FBI – 2023 Internet Crime Report

URL 2023 IC3 Report (PDF)
Author / Organization FBI – Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3)
Abstract In 2023, the FBI’s Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) received 880,418 cybercrime complaints, showing how rapidly online fraud continues to grow. Reported financial losses exceeded $12.5 billion, which represents a 22% increase from 2022. The most frequently reported types of online fraud included investment scams, business email compromise (BEC), and tech-support scams. These schemes show how offenders use deceptive communication tactics and impersonation to exploit victims. The report highlights the continued evolution of online fraud, emphasizing that criminals shift strategies to target individuals across different digital platforms. Overall, the 2023 data demonstrates the significant scale of online fraud and the need for increased awareness and protective measures.

Source 2: IC3 / FBI – 2024 Internet Crime Report

URL 2024 IC3 Report (PDF)
Author / Organization FBI – Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3)
Abstract According to the 2024 IC3 annual report, financial losses linked to cybercrime reached a record-breaking $16.6 billion, marking a 33% increase from the previous year. A large portion of this total came from cyber-enabled fraud, including phishing and spoofing attacks, extortion schemes, and intrusions involving stolen personal data. The report indicates that criminals continue refining their methods to deceive victims and exploit vulnerabilities in both personal and organizational systems. These upward trends highlight how widespread and costly online fraud has become, stressing the importance of improved cybersecurity practices and greater public education to help reduce victimization.
