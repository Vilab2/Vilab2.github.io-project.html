### Subtopic 1: Online Fraud and Scams **Source 1: IC3 / FBI – 2023 Internet Crime Report** **URL**: 2023 IC3 Report (PDF) **Author / Organization**: FBI – Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) **Abstract**: In 2023, the FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center stated that there were over 880,000 cybercrime complaints, with financial losses surpassing $12.5 billion, a 22% increase from the previous year. Common scams included investment fraud, business email compromise, and tech-support scams. These findings show how online fraud methods continue to evolve as criminals adapt to exploit users across multiple digital platforms. The scale of reported losses reinforces the need for increased public awareness and stronger protective measures to reduce victimization. --- **Source 2: IC3 / FBI – 2024 Internet Crime Report** **URL**: 2024 IC3 Report (PDF) **Author / Organization**: FBI – Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) **Abstract**: The 2024 Internet Crime Complaint Center report revealed that losses from cybercrime reached $16.6 billion, a 33% increase from 2023. Much of this came from cyber-enabled fraud, including phishing, spoofing, and extortion. The report emphasizes that criminals are becoming increasingly sophisticated, exploiting weaknesses in both personal and organizational systems. The rising statistics highlight the need for stronger cybersecurity practices and expanded public education to prevent online victimization.
